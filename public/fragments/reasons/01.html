I understand remote work

I've been working remotely on a full time basis since 2014. Here's what I've learnt during this time.

  • I always try to communicate asynchronously. Whilst it's important to interrupt someone in an emergency, this is a rare event, and can be destructive to a developer's focus.
  • I take time to create thoughtful messages that do not criticise or antagonise. I try to avoid the use of emotive words and phrases such as you or can't you just.
  • I appreciate that whilst I can often work anywhere or any time, it's important to be available during 'office hours' should somebody need to contact me.
  • I've created a home office where I can separate work from life. It is a quiet space where I can fully concentrate on so-called 'deep' work.
  • I make sure my broadband connection is fast and stable. Time spent waiting for files to download or diagnosing video calling issues is better spent elsewhere.
  • I switch off at the end of the day. I have learned that the best work cannot be carried out when stressed and overworked.
